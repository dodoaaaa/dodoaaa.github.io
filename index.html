Current Research

I am currently developing quadruped manipulators capable of precise and robust operation in unstructured environments. This work addresses hardware limitations, such as structural compliance, and software challenges including multi-task execution and long-horizon trajectory optimization. I leverage reinforcement learning to improve locomotion and manipulation performance, laying the foundation for extending these methods to aerial and humanoid mobile manipulators in future research.
